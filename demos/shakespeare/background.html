Background

Birthdate/Death & Burial -
Birth: April 23, 1564
Death: April 23, 1616 (The cause of death is not known, but some scholars believe that he was sick for over a month before he died.)
Burial: In the chancel of Holy Trinity Church in his hometown of Stratford, Warwickshire.
Family & Marriage -
Shakespeare's Mother: Mary Shakespeare
Shakespeare's Father: John Shakespeare
Married to: Anne Hathaway
Shakespeare's Children: Usanna, Hamnet and Judith
Education -
Shakespeare began his education at the age of 6 or 7 at Stratford grammar school.
He didn't attended a university.
Career -
As an actor: Shakespeare has been recruited by the Leicester's or Queen's men. An acting troupe recruited Shakespeare in his hometown or he was forced on his own to travel to London to begin his career, he was nevertheless an established actor in the great city by the end of 1592. The theatres had been closed since 1592 beacuse of an outbreak of the plague. He left the theatre entirely during this time to work on his non-dramatic poetry. He had worked as an actor which enhanced his reputation as a Poet.
As an playwright: Shakespeare also moved to London to pursue a career as a playwright. Shakespeare wrote 38 plays, 154 sonnets, and two narrative poems.
The Globe -
The Globe Theatre was a circular wooden structure constructed of three stories of seats, surrounding an open courtyard. There was no roof, and a rectangular platform projected into the middle of the courtyard to serve as a stage.
The performance space had no front curtain, but was backed by a large wall with three doors out of where actors entered and exited. In front of the wall stood a roofed house-like structure supported by two large pillars.
Materials to build The Globe: Timber, nails, stone (flint), plaster, and thatched roofs.
Carpenter Peter Smith and his workers built thhis most magnificent theater that London had ever seen.
Plays that showed: As You Like It, Hamlet, Measure for Measure, Othello, King Lear, Macbeth, and Antony and Cleopatra.
Actors: They generally wore the dress of their own time. Some were specifically made for the actors and some were donated by rich patrons.
Sets: Because settings on an Elizabethan stage were spare, Shakespeare had to write descriptions of them into his dialogue. He wrote his best descriptions.
Seating for the audience: The richer members of the audience were placed in the balconies that overlook the Globe theater.
People who did not have as much money or were not as fortunate had to sit next to the polygon shaped floor.
Actors: William Shakespeare, Edward Alleyn, Robert Armin, Christopher Beeston,
