Self Portrait (Winter 2012)

Using Adobe Illustrator CS6, I had to trace a picture of myself and create myself as a cartoon.

First, I went into Photo-booth to take a picture of myself before I could start tracing myself on Illustrator. When I was interested in my picture, I opened up Illustrator and imported the JPEG file/picture (the picture to the right). I selected the pen tool and started tracing myself on-top of the JPEG picture I imported.
Pretty much everything, such as the head, hair, eyes, nose mouth, body and clothes had to be on their own layer so editing can be a little easier when we wanted to make changes on anything.
After I was done tracing myself and adding color with the pen tool, I took the 1st layer (the original JPEG picture of myself) and created a background layer. I wanted my background to match the Hello Kitty jacket I had on, so I made red flowers.

Photoshop Inspiration (Spring 2013)

Using Adobe Photoshop CS6, I gathered many pictures and items that inspires me.

After watching the inspirational video "Big Spaceship" in class, we were assigned to create something that inspires us.
It was required to have at least 5 items or more.
I took photos of my melted crayon canvas, guitar, and my dog. Also I drew the next 3 remaining cartoon characters in my sketchbook and scanned them in the scanner. I then took the 3 drawings, traced them with the pen-tool in Photoshop, and colored them. Lastly, I took everything, made them look like stickers because of the white outline, and put them all together in the melted crayon canvas background.

Sketchbook Assignment (Spring 2013)

For this sketchbook assignment, we had to create a drawing that looks like it has taken at least 1 or more hours to do, and you could choose any topic on the desired list, such as Inside vs. Outside, Close-Up Abstraction, and many more. The topic I chose for this certain sketchbook assignment was Close-Up Abstraction.
I didn't really know what I was drawing until I was done. I looked at it and thought it was a robotic-starfish. Some people thought it was something else because of the abstract.

This year, I thought this sketchbook assignment was the best I've ever done, because I tried really hard and I worked a long time to draw it, even thought I didn't know what I was drawing until the very end.
This sketchbook drawing was put into Work Ethic because I did take the time to draw this picture and I was happy with the outcome of it.

Andy Warhol (Fall 2014)

Propaganda Poster (Fall 2014)

Using Adobe Illustrator CS6 and our research skills, we were required to create a propaganda poster to inspire people in the 1920’s to have women rights.

I decided to have my poster have a topic of having women rights in work because women do not get all the rights that men have, such as having many more work hours, no breaks, and barely any food and water.

I used Illustrator CS6 to create my poster. Illustrator CS6 is not only used for Web Design classes or for websites. I use it to create illustrations obviously and and many different classes such as history.
